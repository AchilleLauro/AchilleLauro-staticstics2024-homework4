Statistical Independence in Probability Theory

The concept of statistical independence in probability refers to a relationship between two events where the occurrence of one event does not impact the likelihood of the other occurring. If two events are independent, the probability that they happen simultaneously is simply the product of their individual probabilities. Formally, two events \( A \) and \( B \) are independent if:

\[ P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B) \]
